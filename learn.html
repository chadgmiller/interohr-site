How it works

Train perception, regulation, and reflection together.

InteroHR is not just a heart-rate display. It is a practice system. The experience combines body awareness, objective feedback, and pattern tracking so the user can gradually improve how accurately they sense internal state.

01

Notice

Start by pausing and checking in. Instead of relying on the screen immediately, the user first pays attention to internal cues such as rhythm, arousal, steadiness, and bodily tension.

02

Compare

The app compares the estimate with a live or recorded heart-rate value. This creates a clear feedback loop that helps the brain recalibrate over repeated sessions.

03

Improve

Over time, users can identify trends such as underestimating, overestimating, stronger contexts, weaker contexts, and whether recovery ability is building in a meaningful way.

What you are training

Four core dimensions of body awareness.

The app language on the Insights and Index screens maps practice into practical signals that are easier to understand than raw numbers alone.

Calibration

How close your estimate is to what your body was actually doing.

Bias

Whether you tend to underestimate or overestimate your internal state.

Consistency

Whether your awareness is stable from session to session rather than random.

Context Coverage

Whether your skill holds up across resting, stress, caffeine, exercise, and other contexts.

Strong interoception is not just “feeling your body.” It is the ability to notice internal signals, interpret them accurately, and respond with more control.
Science behind InteroHR

Why interoception matters.

Interoception is often described as the sense of the internal state of the body. It influences emotional awareness, self-regulation, stress response, and how people interpret signals like effort, activation, calmness, and recovery.

Better awareness can support more accurate self-monitoring.
Practice with feedback may improve calibration over time.
Recovery-focused practice can help users build regulation habits, not just collect data.
Two main training modes

One for awareness, one for downshifting.

The current app experience teaches both perception and regulation, which work best when practiced together.

Pulse Check

Pulse Check asks a simple but powerful question: how well can you estimate your own heart rate without relying on the screen first? That repeated comparison helps strengthen internal calibration.

  • Choose a context such as resting or another condition.
  • Estimate first, then compare against actual heart rate.
  • Save the entry and let the app build your pattern over time.

Recovery Challenge

Recovery Challenge shifts from perception to regulation. The goal is not to chase a perfect score, but to practice how quickly and steadily you can bring your system back down after activation.

  • Set a drop target and duration.
  • Use breathing or other calming strategies.
  • Review whether recovery is getting stronger over time.
